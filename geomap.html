geo-map-v2

Python · github.com/harrisonwolf/geo-map-v2

Raw sources are adapted, recorded in a hashed canonical ledger with full provenance, frozen into monthly snapshots, scored, and published; the published analytics are immutable while they are served, so every number a client sees traces back to a permanent, tamper-evident record. New regions, sources, and verticals bolt on through the county registry without touching the core; Fresno County is the region built out so far.

The whole stack is Python and SQLite. Four external Python dependencies — shapely for geometry, WeasyPrint for the PDF reports, Pillow, and packaging — and everything else is the standard library. The map client is vendored MapLibre GL; there is no npm, no node, no cloud service, and no external database anywhere in the system.

Fresno County, parcel by parcel: every dot is one parcel, and the map is just the front end to precomputed scores.
The same data three zooms down (every dot a parcel on a named street), and one parcel opened: APN, neighborhood cell, modeled price, with the data-quality label right beside it.
The engine: one make refresh-county run, end to end. 26,000 source rows through adapt → normalize → snapshot (55 ZIPs, 663 cells) → score → a published bundle, with per-stage timings. Click to read.
