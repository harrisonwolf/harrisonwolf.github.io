planar-geometry-engine

C++ · github.com/harrisonwolf/planar-geometry-engine

It solves geometry's classic hard problems: cutting complicated shapes into triangles, and turning a scatter of points into the mesh and region structures that games and mapping software rely on.

The near-touch magnified: the raw polygon (left) and its triangulation (right). The two edges pass about 0.06 units apart and never cross.
One run through the engine at a legible scale (70 sites, seed 7): the raw input, its Delaunay triangulation (125 triangles, 194 edges — Euler-exact), the Voronoi dual, and the two overlaid. Every Voronoi vertex is the circumcenter of a triangle (often outside its own triangle: only acute triangles contain their circumcenters).
And at density: 300 sites, triangulated and dualized end to end by the engine. Seed and command are embedded in the file; the counts check exactly against Euler's formula (581 triangles, 880 edges).

Full detail — build, algorithms, tests — in the README: github.com/harrisonwolf/planar-geometry-engine
