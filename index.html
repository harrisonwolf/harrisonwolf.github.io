boij-soderberg-engine

C++ · ~2,700 lines · hand-written · strongest solo proof

TL;DR — A C++ engine I hand-wrote to test two Betti-number lower-bound conjectures and search for counterexamples. It ran searches Macaulay2 couldn't finish; the results fed a published paper.

  • Computes pure Betti tables via the Herzog–Kuhl equations and sweeps degree-sequence space with per-sequence violation diagnostics.
  • An overflow-avoiding early-exit: grows the scaling factor incrementally and bails the moment it clears a threshold, so exact-integer arithmetic never overflows mid-search.
  • Benchmarked honestly — ~26–46× faster end-to-end (up to ~50×), ~20× lower peak memory — with a method that also ran my algorithms inside Macaulay2 to isolate algorithm from language.
